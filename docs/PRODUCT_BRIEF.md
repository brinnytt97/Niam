# Product Brief

## Product
Niam — iOS app for food discovery, kitchen inventory management,
nutrition tracking, intermittent fasting, and hydration monitoring.

## Primary users
Health-conscious young adults (18-35) who:
- Cook at home regularly
- Want to track what they eat without it feeling like a chore
- Practice or are curious about intermittent fasting
- Want recipe inspiration based on what they already have

## Core user problem
"I open my fridge and don't know what to cook. When I do cook,
I want to know if I'm eating the right amount without tedious logging."

## Current stage
Prototype → MVP (Phase 1: Local Polish)

## Current milestone
**Phase 1: Complete all local features to a usable, polished state.**
The app should feel complete as a standalone offline tool before
adding any cloud/social features.

## Success means
- A new user can set up their profile, add fridge items, create a recipe,
  and get a meal recommendation within 5 minutes
- Daily calorie tracking takes less than 30 seconds per meal
- The app feels warm, not clinical
- Ready for TestFlight beta testing

## Product principles
- Fast to open and use (< 2 seconds to log a meal)
- Warm and low-pressure (not a strict diet app)
- Clear nutrition information (not overwhelming data)
- Works fully offline (cloud features are additive, not required)
- No forced registration (all local features work without an account)

## Not now (Phase 2+)
- Cloud recipe sharing / community
- Social features (follow, profile pages)
- Shared household kitchens
- AI-generated recipe images
- Multiple kitchen support
- Revenue / monetization

## Current constraints
- Platform: iOS 17+ (iPhone required, Mac optional)
- Solo founder + AI development
- Budget: $99/year (Apple Developer) + $0 server costs for Phase 1
- No backend until Phase 2 (Supabase free tier)
